The Challenge

"We know we change lives" is not the same as "we can prove value credibly." Nonprofits create real, profound outcomes — confidence, safety, mentorship, academic persistence — but when it's time to ask for funding, the proof system falls apart. Stories describe fragments. Dashboards capture activities, not value. This is a systems problem, not a storytelling problem. Better slides don't fix it.

The Methodology — Four Moves
  • Capture — primary research into participant experience described in their own words — real human signal before institutional filtering
  • Translate — synthesis normalizing across stakeholder perspectives; turning "confidence" and "belonging" into structured evidence
  • Visualize — executive-legible impact pathway the CEO could walk through live, in a grant meeting, without a designer in the room
  • Operationalize — proof system repeatable by the organization independently — a capability, not a deliverable
"The methodology doesn't flatten human outcomes into numbers. It builds a pathway from lived experience to investment-justifiable value — and keeps both visible at once."
Cara A. Brown
What Was Built

System Map — end-to-end view of how the organization's work creates value, surfacing assumptions rather than implying them. Synthesis Framework — repeatable process so every new cohort feeds the same structure. Interactive Evidence Site — live proof library sponsors could open in a grant meeting, no deck required. Used in the CEO's TED Talk.

The Challenge

Thousands of alumni, donors, and partners — with no reliable way to reach them. The CEO was calling people on her cell phone to fill events. Every relationship lived in her head. Without cohesion, even legitimate impact is undecipherable. Funders couldn't validate nor valuate. Partners couldn't orient. A new facility created both the pressure and the opportunity for a full reset — enabling $35M+ in resources secured within 8 months.

The Approach
  • —Turned a dormant supporter list into a functioning engagement network — replacing grassroots outreach with a scalable system, freeing the CEO from being the only point of contact for thousands
  • —Interdepartmental ways of working + training — the art of critique replaced criticism and unified language increased team cohesion
  • —Elevated the CEO from den mother to accomplished nonprofit executive — a leadership platform, not a cell phone
  • —IA redesigned — funders validate impact easily, community navigates intuitively, alumni engage consistently
  • —New facility launch designed as a full brand and community activation moment
The System

The identity, the content, the navigation, the programs, the events, the leader — these are not aesthetic decisions. They are the conditions under which stakeholders decide whether to engage, whether to fund, whether to trust. The system built here gave the organization a front door, a program suite, a leadership platform, and a partner ecosystem that could finally operationalize the magnitude of what was happening inside.

The Challenge

Oakland's illegal dumping problem: complex, systemic, deeply felt by residents, and resistant to standard policy responses. The facilitation challenge was as much about building shared working conditions across governments as it was about guiding content.

Participants had different government contexts, working styles, and levels of familiarity with design methods. The goal wasn't a good workshop — it was a team that could think differently, and a solution a city could actually use.

How I Facilitated
  • Observed and leaned into each participant's strengths to best guide them through problem framing → research → synthesis → concept → pitch — harmonizing their efforts
  • Applied human-centered research methods in real time against the actual problem — not theory in isolation
  • Made tradeoffs explicit at every decision point — tested concepts against real operational constraints before the pitch
  • Refined the final pitch for a non-design audience: the Mayor and city leadership needed to see immediate civic value
"The facilitation was the design work — structuring process, making tradeoffs legible, and shaping a pitch that could land with city leadership."

The Challenge

Two enterprises. Incompatible operating models. No shared language for what had to change — or when. Every pillar had its own complexity and its own stakeholders protecting their turf. What was missing wasn't effort. It was architecture.

The Approach
  • 4-fidelity level (4F) blueprint framework — from hypothesis sketches to fully detailed, socialization-ready artifacts with embedded decision logs
  • Hypothesis-led workshops across both organizations to surface ground truth and force decision points
  • Managed 5 design counterparts simultaneously across both enterprises
  • Repeatable workflow applied to each integration pillar with consistent rigor
"Seeing things at enterprise-wide scale — and telescoping down to nuance without losing either — is the rare skill that makes Design Strategists irreplaceable in integration work."
Cara A. Brown
The Blueprint System

The 4F blueprint was the definitive final artifact — all decisions, who made them, when, and who participated. Documentation is now accountability infrastructure. This is the deliverable standard a strategist embedded in complex projects expect from a strategist embedded in complex projects.

The Challenge

Transportation was bleeding money visible on spreadsheets — but leadership couldn't pinpoint the reasons on the ground, because staff put on their best face and gave the right answers. A Supply Chain VP stopped a strategy meeting mid-presentation: "I don't know what you're talking about." Three years of initiative stall had produced no financial case and no field truth.

The Approach
  • Visited 5 distribution centers — replaced untested assumptions with validated on-the-ground evidence: $15M confirmed lost at a single site in one visit
  • Translated operational reality into financial language — one-page insight snapshot the VP printed, read in the room, and acted on immediately
  • Built a custom multi-tab product requirements framework mapping field findings directly to specs — creating team velocity
  • Built the Collaborative Thinking Dojo: charter, research, and requirements in one shared source of truth
