Hiring Committee
HGA Architects and Engineers
Director of Design Insight

The role of Director of Design Insight at HGA describes something I have been building toward for fifteen years — and something I have, in many ways, already been doing. My career has been defined by one consistent practice: going to where people actually are, understanding how they experience the systems around them, and translating that understanding into organizational change that lasts. That is the work HGA is asking this Director to lead. I would like to be considered for it.

My background is not in architecture, but it is in the discipline that sits underneath it — the design of the systems, experiences, and organizational conditions that determine whether a built environment actually functions for the people inside it. At Kroger, I designed the organization's first enterprise-wide human experience research and measurement program across 34 distribution centers. I went to the ground, conducted contextual inquiry, synthesized qualitative and quantitative findings, and built a measurement architecture that was subsequently institutionalized by the EVP of Supply Chain as a permanent operational health metric. NPS moved from −36 to +49. The program was presented at the Qualtrics Conference. That is not a UX project. That is what happens when research is taken seriously as organizational infrastructure.

At EOYDC, I designed Mapping Success — a repeatable research methodology for translating qualitative human outcomes into funder-credible evidence. The CEO adopted it as her primary decision-support tool in every grant meeting, including her TED Talk. It supported a $500K PropelNext grant — one of the most competitive in the nonprofit sector. This is the kind of work HGA's Director of Design Insight is asked to build: research that becomes organizational capability, not just a deliverable.

I understand that HGA's context is the built environment, and I want to address that directly. The throughline of my work is not a single industry — it is a method: understand how people interact with environments, systems, and services; identify where those environments create friction, exclusion, or failure; and design the organizational and physical conditions that produce better outcomes. I have done this in enterprise supply chains, post-merger integrations, civic institutions, community organizations, and regulated operational environments. The translation to the built environment is not a leap. The questions are the same. What does this space ask of the people inside it? What does it prevent? What does it make possible?

I am also a practice builder. Through Alanne International, my 20+ year independent consultancy, I have led engagements across 25+ clients — developing proprietary methodology, building tools and frameworks, and translating research into repeatable, teachable practice. I know what it takes to build a transdisciplinary capability from the inside of an organization, and I know how to recruit for and develop it. I would bring that experience to DIG.

What draws me specifically to HGA is the seriousness of the investment. A Director of Design Insight who reports to market leaders, collaborates with a Digital Insight Group, and is expected to shape firmwide research and innovation practice — that is not a support function. That is a strategic leadership role. I am ready for it, and I believe my portfolio demonstrates why.

I would welcome the opportunity to speak further. My portfolio and case studies are available at caraabrown.com.
